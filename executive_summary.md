# Executive Summary: European Banking Churn Analytics

## Purpose

This project identifies which European banking customer groups are most likely to churn and where the bank faces the highest financial exposure. The analysis supports targeted retention, better customer protection, and more efficient marketing decisions.

## Main Results

- The overall churn rate is 20.4%, equal to 2,037 churned customers out of 10,000.
- Germany is the highest-risk region, with a churn rate of 32.4%.
- Customers aged 46-60 show the highest churn rate at 51.1%.
- Inactive customers churn at 26.9%, compared with 14.3% for active customers.
- High-balance customers churn at 25.2%, creating approximately EUR 159.5M in high-value balance exposure.
- Total balance held by churned customers is approximately EUR 185.6M.

## Policy and Management Implications

Banks should not treat churn as a single portfolio-level metric. The highest risks are concentrated in specific regions, age groups, engagement levels, and high-value balance segments. This means broad retention campaigns may waste resources while missing the customers who matter most.

## Recommended Actions

1. Prioritize Germany for regional churn review and customer experience improvement.
2. Create a high-value customer retention program for customers with balances above EUR 100,000.
3. Use inactivity as an early warning signal and trigger reactivation campaigns.
4. Build age-specific retention strategies for customers aged 46-60.
5. Monitor churn through the Streamlit dashboard using dynamic segment filters and drill-down tables.

## Deliverables

- Reproducible data preparation script.
- Cleaned segmentation dataset.
- Interactive Streamlit analytics dashboard.
- Research paper with EDA findings and recommendations.
